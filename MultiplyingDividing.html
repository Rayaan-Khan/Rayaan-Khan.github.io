MULTIPLYING & DIVIDING POSITIVE AND NEGATIVE NUMBERS

multiplying or dividing is preformed the same way as normal whole numbers, except you must keep track of the signs. To multiply or divide integers with a negative, always multiply or divide the absolute value and then use the rules to determine if the answer is negative or positive


When you multiply two integers with the same signs, the answer will always be positive. Simply multiply the absolute values and make it positive.

Positive x positive = positive 
Negative x negative = positive

When you multiply two integers with different signs, the result will always be negative. Simply multiply the absolute values and add the negative symbol next to the number

Positive x negative = negative
Negative x positive = negative

When you are dividing two integers with the same sign, the result is always positive. Just divide the absolute values like normal.

Positive ÷ positive = positive
Negative ÷ negative = positive

When you divide two integers with different signs, the result will always be negative. So simply divide the absolute values and add the negative symbol to your answer.

Positive ÷ negative = negative
Negative ÷ positive = negative

Examples:

  • -5 × -4 = 20
  • -3 × 8 = -24
  • -10÷15 = 2
  • -12÷4 = -3
