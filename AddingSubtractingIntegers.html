ADDING AND SUBTRACTING POSITIVE AND NEGATIVE INTEGERS

Adding and subtracting integers forms the very basics of algebra, so it is an important topic to thoroughly understand, because many equations and formulas will involve adding and subtracting integers. Integers refers to numbers which can either be positive or negative.

  • Positive integers have values greater than zero.
  • Negative integers have values less than zero.
  • Zero is neither positive nor negative.

Addition

With that clear there is another set of rules, which tells us how positive and negative react to each other, when adding and adding

  • Positive plus positive = positive (20+23 = 43)
  • Negative plus Negative = Negative (-20+-23 = -43)
  • Negative plus positive = Negative (-9+3 = -6)
  • Positive plus Negative = Negative (15+-10 = 5)

So for example if we are adding 9+ -3 it will simply become 9-3 because the rules state positive plus negative equal to negative. Let’s look at this on a number line. If we do

5+ -3 we can simplify to 5-3 making it easier to understand.

Subtraction

The rules for subtraction are similar to those for addition. If you have two positive integers, you would subtract the smaller number from the larger one. The result will always be a positive integer:

  • 5 – 3 = 2

In like manner, if you were to subtract a positive integer from a negative one, the calculation becomes a matter of addition (with the addition of a negative value):

  • (–5) – 3 = –5 + (–3) = –8

If you're subtracting negatives from positives, the two negatives cancel out and it becomes addition:

  • 5 – (–3) = 5 + 3 = 8

If you're subtracting a negative from another negative integer, use the sign of the larger number and subtract:

  • (–5) – (–3) = (–5) + 3 = –2
  • (–3) – (–5) = (–3) + 5 = 2

If you get confused, it often helps to write a positive number in an equation first and then the negative number. This can make it easier to see whether a sign change occurs.
